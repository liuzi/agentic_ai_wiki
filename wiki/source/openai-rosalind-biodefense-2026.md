**Citation:** OpenAI Team. "Strengthening societal resilience with Rosalind Biodefense." OpenAI Blog, 2026. [URL](https://openai.com/index/strengthening-societal-resilience-with-rosalind-biodefense/) | [Local copy](../../raw/2026/openai/Strengthening%20societal%20resilience%20with%20Rosalind%20Biodefense.md)

**Claim:** Expanding trusted access to GPT-Rosalind for vetted developers and government partners represents a "defensive acceleration" strategy that ensures frontier AI capabilities meaningfully advantage those building society's defenses against biological threats.

**Method:** Two-pronged approach: (1) Rosalind Biodefense program providing sponsored access to GPT-Rosalind for trusted developers building defensive applications, and (2) extending trusted access to select U.S. government and allied partners with approved public health and biodefense missions.

**Results:** 
- Launched partnerships with Fourth Eon Biosecurity (AI-native screening systems), Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (biopreparedness platform), Johns Hopkins APL (protein engineering), and CEPI (vaccine development)
- Structured access controls with safety, security, and accountability measures
- Applications span epidemiological modeling, early detection, screening, preparedness, and medical countermeasures

**What's new:** This represents the first large-scale implementation of "defensive acceleration" as an AI governance philosophy—ensuring dual-use capabilities preferentially benefit defenders. The trusted access model creates structured pathways for high-stakes AI deployment with explicit safety controls.

**What I'd push on:** Limited details on the actual capability improvements GPT-Rosalind provides over existing tools. No concrete metrics on defensive advantage or timeline for impact assessment. The selection criteria for "trusted" partners could be more transparent.

**Touches:** Links to dual-use AI governance, trusted access models, defensive acceleration strategy, GPT-Rosalind capabilities, and AI safety in high-stakes domains.
