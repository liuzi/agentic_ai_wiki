# Frontier Research Capabilities

A thread tracking AI systems' ability to contribute to cutting-edge research across scientific domains.

---

## 2025-01-14 | First detailed mechanistic analysis of frontier model

Anthropic publishes "Biology of a Large Language Model," providing the first detailed mechanistic interpretability analysis of a frontier production model (Claude 3.5 Haiku) rather than toy models.

**Key developments:**
- Attribution graphs reveal sophisticated internal mechanisms: multi-step reasoning, forward/backward planning, primitive metacognitive circuits
- Evidence of abstract computational mechanisms that generalize across domains
- First scalable circuit tracing methodology that works on production-scale models
- Discovery of internal planning and reasoning capabilities not visible in model outputs

**Significance:** Demonstrates that frontier models have rich internal structure amenable to scientific analysis. Shows AI research methodology advancing to keep pace with model capabilities—moving from toy model analysis to production model mechanistic understanding.

**Source:** [Anthropic Biology LLM](../source/anthropic-biology-llm-2025.md)

## 2026-06-01 | First AI-solved mathematical theorem

OpenAI announces that a general-purpose reasoning model has autonomously solved the 80-year-old Erdős unit distance problem, marking the first time AI has independently resolved a prominent open problem in mathematics.

**Key developments:**
- Model disproved longstanding conjecture using algebraic number theory techniques
- External mathematicians verified the proof independently  
- Test-time compute scaling: 0% → 48% success rate with 16x compute
- Solution surprised experts by connecting distant mathematical domains

**Significance:** Demonstrates AI moving from research assistance to autonomous discovery. The cross-domain insight (algebraic number theory → discrete geometry) suggests models can identify non-obvious connections that human experts missed.

**Source:** [OpenAI Discrete Geometry](../source/openai-discrete-geometry-2026.md)

## 2026-05-20 | Defensive acceleration in biology

OpenAI launches structured access to GPT-Rosalind for biodefense applications, implementing "defensive acceleration" philosophy to ensure dual-use AI capabilities preferentially benefit defenders.

**Key developments:**
- Rosalind Biodefense program for vetted developers building defensive tools
- Trusted access for government partners in public health/biodefense  
- Partnerships span screening systems, medical countermeasures, vaccine development
- First large-scale implementation of defensive acceleration governance

**Significance:** Shows transition from general AI capabilities to domain-specific deployment with governance frameworks. Suggests recognition that frontier models require structured access rather than broad deployment in sensitive domains.

**Source:** [OpenAI Rosalind Biodefense](../source/openai-rosalind-biodefense-2026.md)

## Research Questions

**Convergence vs. Divergence:** Are we seeing AI capabilities converge toward general research competence, or diverge into domain-specific tools like GPT-Rosalind?

**Access Patterns:** How will the tension between open research and defensive acceleration resolve as capabilities increase?

**Discovery Mechanisms:** What makes AI effective at cross-domain insights? Is this scaling with compute, training, or architectural changes?

**Human-AI Collaboration:** How is the division of labor evolving between AI systems doing discovery and humans doing interpretation/prioritization?
