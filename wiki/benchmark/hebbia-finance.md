# Hebbia Finance Benchmark

## What it measures

The Hebbia Finance Benchmark evaluates senior-level financial reasoning capabilities including document-based reasoning, chart and table interpretation, and complex problem solving in financial contexts. Designed to test the analytical skills required for high-level finance work rather than basic financial knowledge.

## Methodology

Created by Hebbia, this benchmark focuses on complex analytical tasks that mirror senior-level financial work. Key evaluation areas include:
- **Document-based reasoning**: Extracting insights from financial documents and reports
- **Chart and table interpretation**: Analyzing visual financial data presentations
- **Problem solving**: Complex financial analysis and decision-making scenarios

The benchmark appears designed to test practical financial analysis skills rather than theoretical knowledge.

## Known flaws and contamination concerns

- **Proprietary benchmark**: Limited public information about test cases and methodology
- **Domain specificity**: May not generalize to other analytical reasoning domains
- **Evaluation subjectivity**: Senior-level reasoning tasks may involve subjective judgment calls
- **Limited baseline data**: As a newer benchmark, fewer comparison points available

## What a meaningful delta looks like

For a senior-level reasoning benchmark, meaningful improvements likely involve:
- **Analytical depth**: Better insight extraction from complex financial documents
- **Integration ability**: Combining information from multiple sources (documents, charts, tables)
- **Practical applicability**: Solutions that would be actionable in real financial contexts
- **Accuracy under uncertainty**: Sound reasoning when data is incomplete or conflicting

Given the focus on senior-level work, improvements need to demonstrate sophisticated reasoning rather than just factual recall.
