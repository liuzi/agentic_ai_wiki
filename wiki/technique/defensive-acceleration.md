# Defensive Acceleration

## TL;DR

Defensive acceleration ensures frontier AI capabilities meaningfully advantage defenders over potential bad actors. Rather than broad deployment or restrictive access, this approach provides structured pathways for vetted organizations working on defensive applications while maintaining safety controls.

## Mechanism

The technique involves several key components:
- **Trusted access models**: Vetting process for organizations seeking access to sensitive capabilities
- **Defensive use case prioritization**: Preferential access for applications that strengthen societal defenses
- **Structured deployment**: Controlled release with monitoring and enforcement mechanisms
- **Safety-security integration**: Layered safeguards appropriate for dual-use capabilities

Implementation requires balancing access (enabling beneficial applications) with control (preventing misuse), typically through partnerships with government agencies, research institutions, and mission-aligned organizations.

## Lineage

**Descends from:** Dual-use research governance, responsible disclosure practices in cybersecurity, and export control frameworks for sensitive technologies.

**Competes with:**
- Open-source deployment strategies that maximize broad access
- Restrictive access models that limit capability deployment entirely
- Commercial deployment focused on market rather than defensive priorities

**Related to:** AI safety research emphasizing alignment and robustness for high-stakes applications.

## Caveats & Failure modes

- **Access bottlenecks**: Vetting processes may slow legitimate defensive research
- **Definition ambiguity**: "Defensive" vs "offensive" applications can be context-dependent
- **Competitive disadvantage**: Restrictive access may advantage less scrupulous actors
- **Governance complexity**: Requires sophisticated evaluation of use cases and actors
- **International coordination**: Effectiveness depends on alignment across jurisdictions

## Open questions

- How do we scale vetting processes without creating unacceptable delays for defenders?
- What criteria reliably distinguish defensive from dual-use applications?
- How does defensive acceleration interact with international AI governance frameworks?
- Can this approach work for capabilities that are inherently dual-use?
- What are the long-term competitive dynamics between defensive and open deployment strategies?
